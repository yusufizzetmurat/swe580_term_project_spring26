# January 27, 2026

Two meetings today — busy but productive.

[[Collaboration_Meeting_Jan27]] with Dr. Park's group was the most concrete meeting we've had. Data sharing happened, Marcus offered to review the multilingual approach, and we have a real timeline now. The February 3 deadline for sharing chatbot API access is tight but doable.

The [[Transfer_Learning]] notes were the main reading project this afternoon. Writing these helped me articulate something I've been taking for granted: the reason fine-tuning works at scale is that most of what a model needs to know about language is already encoded in the pre-training weights. The task-specific fine-tuning is just the last mile.

Also added proper [[Bash_Scripting]] notes. The parallel execution pattern (backgrounding Python processes with `wait`) is something I need more often now that the experiment grid is getting large.

After the collaboration meeting, I realized the paper needs a clearer "deployment" angle. It's not just about accuracy — Dr. Park's team immediately asked about latency and whether the chatbot could handle concurrent users. These are real constraints that the [[Knowledge_Distillation]] work addresses. Professor Chen was right to want this in the paper.
